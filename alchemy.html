Alchemy

Control

Trial

Successfully brewing a potion (without overcrafting) allows you to learn the minimum of the success range of the potion.

Careful Control

After rolling to brew a potion, you may either increase or decrease the roll by 2.

Precise Proportioning

When rolling to brew potions, you may choose to increase or decrease either the maximum or minimum of the success range by 2.

Masterful Mixing

After rolling to brew a potion, you may subtract your roll from 20, and take that value as your roll.

Perfect Brew

After rolling to brew a potion, if the roll is exactly equal to the middle of the success range, your potion becomes perfectly brewed, doubling the basic effect of your potion.

Alchemical Chaining

Upon perfectly brewing a potion, if you have the ingredients to do so, you may roll to brew another potion. Add that potion’s effects to your first potion, rather than making a new potion. This effect may repeat multiple times.

Intensity

When perfectly brewing a potion you may choose to treat it as being Overcrafted.

Hypersensitivity

Perfectly brewed potions additionally apply the following effect to creatures it affects: Double the effect of future potions on you until the end of combat. This effect can only apply once per creature.

Error

Overcrafting a potion allows you to learn the maximum of the success range of the potion.

Potency

When Overcrafting a potion, you may choose to double the Overcraft effect. Add another Overcraft Table effect to the potion.

Overenthusiasm

When brewing a potion, gain +20 to your dice roll.

Fixing the odds

You may now select your Overcraft Table effects.
